|## Reference manual<br><br>The official definition of the Lua language is its [reference manual](https://www.lua.org/manual/), which describes the syntax and the semantics of Lua, the standard libraries, and the C API.<br><br>The [reference manuals](https://www.lua.org/manual) for all versions of Lua are [available online](https://www.lua.org/manual) in English. Some are available in other languages and as a printed book.<br><br>The current version is the [reference manual for Lua 5.4](https://www.lua.org/manual/5.4/).<br><br>## Technical documentation<br><br>For more technical information, see the [Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)](https://www.lua.org/faq.html) and some old [seminar slides](https://www.lua.org/slides.html). For detailed technical information on specific topics, see our old series of [Lua Technical Notes](https://www.lua.org/notes/) and the [wiki](http://lua-users.org/wiki/) at [lua-users.org](http://lua-users.org/), specially the [tutorial](http://lua-users.org/wiki/TutorialDirectory). You may also browse the [source code](https://www.lua.org/source/).<br><br>## Papers<br><br>There are many [papers and theses](https://www.lua.org/papers.html) related to Lua.
